1. Sublime Laser: Company Overview

1.1 Company Profile and History

Sublime Laser was founded in 2022 and operates from its headquarters at 1785 South 4130 West, Suite M, Salt Lake City, Utah. The company has raised a total of $2.54 million in funding, with its most recent round occurring two years ago.

Sublime Laser is currently in an unattributed Stage III growth phase and maintains a lean, agile team structure. The company's core business is custom laser machining for the medical device industry, with a specialization in femtosecond laser technology.

The company's services are tailored to produce tight-tolerance components in a variety of metals and materials, emphasizing rapid turnaround from design to production. The company is ISO 13485 certified, underscoring its commitment to quality and regulatory compliance in the medtech sector.

1.6 Market Focus and Industries Served

  • Neurovascular applications

Technical Capabilities and Processes

Sublime Laser's technical offering centers on two primary laser modalities: femtosecond and fiber lasers. The femtosecond lasers vaporize material to create parts with exact dimensions and minimal heat-affected zones (HAZ), a critical requirement for high-precision medical components. The fiber lasers, by contrast, melt material for rapid and accurate part production.

1.3 Materials, Tolerances, and Product Types

Sublime Laser's process expertise encompasses nitinol, stainless steel, precious metals, ceramics, and glass. The company's stated kerf widths and feature sizes (as small as 12 microns) position it at the forefront of microfabrication for medical device components.

3. Technology Comparison

Femtosecond vs. Fiber vs. Ultrashort Pulse Lasers

Femtosecond Lasers

Emit pulses with durations in the femtosecond range (10⁻¹⁵ seconds), enabling material removal with minimal thermal effects.

  • Extremely precise cuts
  • Ultra-fine kerf widths
  • Virtually no HAZ
  • Ideal for stents, hypotubes, microelectrodes
  • Best for nitinol and sensitive alloys

Fiber Lasers

Use optical fibers doped with rare-earth elements to generate high-intensity laser beams. Efficient for rapid material removal.

  • High precision capability
  • Larger HAZ than femtosecond
  • Speed prioritized over ultra-fine resolution
  • Common for cutting, welding, drilling
  • Less thermally sensitive applications

Ultrashort Pulse & Nd:YAG

Ultrashort pulse lasers (picosecond and femtosecond) offer greater control over material interaction.

  • Finer features possible
  • Reduced recast layers
  • Nd:YAG versatile for welding/cutting
  • Can handle metal combinations
  • Application-driven selection
